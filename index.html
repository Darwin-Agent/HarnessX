HarnessX

A Composable, Adaptive, and Evolvable Agent Harness Foundry

Darwin Agent Team · 5 benchmarks · 15 model-benchmark configurations

Read paper GitHub

Overview

Treat the harness as a first-class interface.

HarnessX separates a model from the runtime behavior around it. Prompts, tools, memory, control flow, evaluation, and training export become composable primitives that can improve from execution feedback.

01

Compose

Swap behavior across nine orthogonal dimensions without rewriting the agent.

02

Adapt

Turn traces into bounded harness edits, then test and gate every candidate.

03

Evolve

Convert reward-annotated trajectories into training signal for a better model.

AEGIS

The harness learns from its own traces.

AEGIS digests execution history, plans a targeted change, generates a candidate, and critiques the result. Only verified improvements become the next harness.

Results

Harness evolution improves 14 of 15 configurations.

The evaluation spans GAIA, ALFWorld, WebShop, tau3-Bench, and SWE-bench Verified across three model families. The largest gains appear where the initial model has the most behavioral gaps.

+14.5% average absolute gain across all 15 configurations
+44.0% largest gain, on ALFWorld with Qwen3.5-9B
14 / 15 model-benchmark configurations improved

Co-evolution

Search and learning reinforce each other.

Interleaving cross-harness GRPO with AEGIS adds an average +4.7% over harness-only evolution on GAIA and WebShop.
